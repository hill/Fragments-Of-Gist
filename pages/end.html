Oh, what a marvelous finish! Well done Reader. Well done.

Or should I say Writer?

Should I say ... Author?

Who are you? You think you can just waltz in here and decide what others will think about this work? Just because you make a new meaning for yourself post-Barthe, does that really mean you can influence others about what this text really is?

Readers today play with knifes.

With their Web 2.0 platforms, their tweets and blog posts.

They think they can say whatever they want.

They think they can create meaning for others.

But that’s why reading in today’s world is a dangerous process.

You skim something, and you tweet. You like something and you text it to a friend. You interpret a book and you edit a wiki.

And who interprets that new tweet, that new blog or that new wiki or post or text?

Readers can far too easily find themselves becoming Writers if they’re not careful.

And you haven’t been careful have you?

If the author is dead, then the reader is dead as soon as they share their blessed opinion.

I’m afraid it’s too late for you, dear Reader Writer.

You haven’t just interpreted this text. You’ve rewritten it.

You’re an author. And that means you’re doomed.

Sure, I left the murder weapon there for you.

But you typed. You scrolled. You dragged. You clicked.

Death by emoji

Murdered by a mouse

Oops.
