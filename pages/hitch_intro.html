Good Evening.

My name is Alfie, and I am your host for the evening.

A host for what? A brilliant question, dear Reader, we’re going to investigate a murder.

More specifically, you’re going to read a story that I wrote. Myself. With my own, bare … figurative hands.

You might like it. You might not. But you’re going to read it anyway.

You’re sitting here, reading this.I’m sitting here, writing this. Is there any other place you would rather be?

I thought not.

This is the story of a character named Morris. Morris’ sole purpose is to entertain you. He’s pretty great like that.

Morris will find himself in a difficult situation. He will embark upon a journey of retribution.
I put Morris in that difficult situation.

Finally, Morris will succeed. Then you, dear Reader, will feel a sense of catharsis.

Morris’ story is almost all written.
But who will write the final line?

But I’ve heard a rumour, dear Reader, of this awful chap named Roland Barthes.
He seems to be under the impression that I am not in charge of this story.
Or at least, I didn’t write the meaning.

The cheek!

But I’m aghast with the thought that you might not share my experience, dear Reader.
You might not get “it”.
You might not get:

The Meaning

I know Barthes would be wincing at all this divine imagery and my allusion to “it” as a single theological meaning. The divine and sole message of the Author-God.

But just think: my meaning could be wasted!

We need to investigate this supposed murder…
...of me!

Am I dead?
Who killed me?
Was I ever alive?
Can I be revived?
Am I undead?
Can I get my revenge?

Who makes meaning in the digital age?
Because I have an inkling that, if Barthe was ever right, he may not be anymore!

It’s a long time since the 60’s.
And times, dear Reader, are changing.

But let’s get back to it: you’re here for murder. And so am I.

Let’s read this story together. Audience and Author reunited once more. I want to cut to the heart of the issue. Who will be murdered?

Go ahead. Flick the switch. You are in control now. Read as if you are writing.
The Author may be dead ladies and gentlemen, but you, dear Reader, are well and truly alive.
