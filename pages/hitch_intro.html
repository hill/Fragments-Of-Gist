Good Evening,

Hello there reader. It’s a delightful evening, don’t you think?

You’re sitting here, reading this. I’m sitting here, writing this. Is there any other place you would rather be?

I thought not.

Oh! I almost forgot to introduce myself. My name is Alfie, and I shall be your host for this evening.

A host for what? A brilliant question. Today, dear participant, we’re going to investigate a murder.

More specifically, you’re going to read a story that I wrote. Myself. With my own, bare ... figurative hands.

You might like it. You might not. But you’re going to read it anyway.

This is the story of a character named Morris. Morris’ sole purpose is to entertain you. He’s pretty great like that.

Morris’ name was chosen for a specific reason; it is uncommon enough in the target demographic (only one in eighteen thousand, two hundred and fifty eight!) that it might elicit some kind of memorable experience for the reader.
I have chosen that name for him.

Morris will find himself in a difficult situation. He will embark upon a journey of vengeance and retribution.
I put Morris in that difficult situation.

Finally, Morris will succeed. You, dear reader, will feel a sense of great euphoria after finally receiving resolution. Morris’ quest will be complete. The story will be over.
I have decided that final line.

Morris’ story is already written.
I wrote it.

But I’ve heard rumour, dear reader, of this awful chap named Barthe that seems to be under the impression that I didn’t write it. Or at least, I didn’t write the meaning. That was just tomb much for me, dear associate.

I’m aghast with the thought that you might not share my experience, that you might not get it.
That you wouldn’t get:

The Meaning

I know Barthes would be wincing at all this divine imagery and allusion to "it" as a single theological meaning. The divine and sole message of the Author-God.

But just think: all that meaning. Wasted! Lost!

Murdered.

We need to investigate this supposed murder of me. We need to slice through the rubbish and get to the bottom of this.

Who makes meaning in the modern day? Look outside on this Friday Afternoon! It’s 2017. 40 years later is this perpetrator’s homicide really still relevant?

Let’s read this story together. Audience and author reunited once more. I want to cut to the heart of the issue.

Go ahead. Flick the switch. You are in control now. Read as if you are writing. The author may be dead ladies and gentlemen, but you, dear reader, are well and truly alive.
