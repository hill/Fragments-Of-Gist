Chapter 4

Morris ignored the stupid card and continued on his journey to speak to Lexi.
He would confront her in IKEA.
In public.

The engine revved and the car lurched forward out of the garage. The car rushed through the traffic lights, over the curving hills, flung around the roundabouts and down the main street only to find…

… traffic. Of course. A standstill. Trapped between a Peugeot and a Toyota. Nowhere to go. Morris slumps in agony.

But something caught Morris’ eye. A storefront. A grey exterior with frosted glass windows. A solicitor’s office.

Morris had an idea.

Traffic. There’s nothing worse than sitting in traffic isn’t that right?

I suppose your dog being crushed by unstable home furniture and appliances could be considered worse. But there’s nothing worse at the time then sitting in traffic.
