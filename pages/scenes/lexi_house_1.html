Chapter 5

The furball hissed and scratched as Morris held it at a meter's distance away from his face. Carbucketty. What a dreadful cat.

Lexi loved it of course. But retribution is retribution. Debts must be payed. All action has consequence. You leave a screw out, you must suffer the result. Just like Morris suffered.

Morris strode out the door and plucked a loose brick from the garden wall as he walked out onto the road, cat in hand.

He lays the cat down and knelt down beside it.

Holds it in position with his spare left forearm.

Lifts the brick with his right hand.

Crack.

Carbucketty dies.

Lifts the brick with his right hand.

Crack.

Lifts the brick with his right hand.

Crack.

Morris tore into the corpse.

Blood and bile and bone. Flesh and hair and liver. Lung. Esophagus. Small intestine. Nerve. Spleen. Fat. Diaphragm. Kidney.

Chunks of meat go flying.

Morris drags the limp body across the road. Across the pavement. Across the brickwork. Red calligraphy caresses the concrete.

Well. That was horrific.
What a terrible thing to do.
Psychologically stable people talk about their issues.
Unless they’re personal.
In which case most people don’t.
Maybe we’re not that different from Morris after all.
