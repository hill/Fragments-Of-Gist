Chapter 5

Morris sits opposite the solicitor, across a mahogany desk.

The bald bespectacled man shifted in his seat. He glanced at the characters. The sentences and phrases; the clauses and subclauses.

Morris was going to sue his girlfriend. What was she thinking! Six screws?! Who just leaves six screws out of a perfectly good, perfectly well documented piece of flat pack furniture like that.

Morris would ruin Lexi. He would take everything she once owned and loved and reduce it to dust through the brilliance of the modern legal system!

The legal documentation was drafted.

The solicitor carefully plucked each word to ensure that there could be no misinterpretation at all.

The words formed sentences. The sentences formed paragraphs. The paragraphs formed bricks. The bricks formed walls.

Walls of text.

Walls defending from the intrusion of ambiguity.
