Here we go. No more terrible reader interpretations of tone, intonation and lexical stress. This is like how it used to be done. Traditional storytelling.

However traditional story telling was told over and over again … how will it sound this time round?

But this is not enough. This digital recording of the ‘correct’ intonation and tonal structures - as envisioned by the Author - will never live up to the fluidity of face-to-face narration. This static narrator can not respond to - as David Hermanin ‘Towards Transmedial Narratology’ calls it: diverse inputs.

This narrator can not actively shape the narrative from questions from the Audience, interruptions, requests for explanations, laughter, facial expressions and ‘supportive vocalisations’. The Meaning, in face-to-face narration is, to an extent, created and supported by participants and listeners.

Meaning is lost!

As William Labov argues, narratives of the ‘Performantial Dimension’ and conversational storytelling are no less purposefully created than those of the written word.

Perhaps it is time to carve out the next path, to click the next button, to shape the story, dear Participant.
