The Story of Morris.

Chapter 1

Morris stood at the steps to his apartment. Fifty four steps. Morris had counted many times on the journey back and forth from IKEA to the car and into his apartment.

Morris had recently acquired this apartment. It was nice. Close to town, a minute’s walk to the park where he could throw a frisbee with his Chihuahua named Tank. Was moving into a new place exciting? Yes. Was dealing with your girlfriend's inability to leave the door open for more than thirty seconds incredibly frustrating? Absolutely.

Morris pushed the flat pack with all his might up the stairwell. The concrete bites into the cardboard covering, a fine powder of brown scattered in his trail. Morris’ legs quickly go from tired to shaking as the oblong object rises, centimeter by centimeter. It’s like dragging the Queen Mary through a marsh of molten mars bars. One final push. Heave!

The door. At the top of the staircase. Closed of course. “Lexi! Lexi!” Morris calls. No response. She must have gone back for the second load of “Årstid” lamps and “Ormhassel” teatowels. Damn. One hand on the flat pack, Morris fumbled around his jean pocket. He fishes his keys out, and plugged them into the keyhole and pushes.

Morris let go of the flatpack with a sigh of relief, then regret. The bookshelf was right behind the door. There was no way Morris was going to squeeze that through, no matter how hard he tried. The flat pack let go of Morris. It tumbled down 4 flights of staircase. “Shit”.

What a difficult situation Morris has found himself in! His life has been momentarily inconvenienced by the locking of a door. By poor communicative skills between him and his beloved Lexi. Shared understanding sadly just was not acheived. But already I feel like the story is getting a little dry. How about we spice it up.

The dog is going to have to die.
