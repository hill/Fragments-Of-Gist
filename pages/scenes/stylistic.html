Chapter 5

The traffic finally cleared. Morris was on his way! He slammed on the accelerator, his foot practically touching the floor.

He arrived IKEA and entered the building. It was time to find Lexi. He ventured down the predestined path so clearly laid out for him. The yellow arrows scattered across the floor would lead him to her. To his revenge.

Morris walked past the countless variations of bed linen, Nordvalla. Dining tables. Mörbylånga. A spoon. A lamp.

He walked past countless shoppers, all of them looking at the same thing, but seeing different things.

And then he finds her.

I feel this is getting a little bland. We need to change up the style.

Why don't we imitate some of the greats. Shakespeare. He'll do. Let's copy and paste, mix and match until his creation, his meaning, becomes my own. Or your own.

According to Barthes, that's what I'm doing already:

"The text is a tissue of citations"
